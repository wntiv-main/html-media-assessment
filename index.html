CHS eSports

    Who runs it?

    CHS eSports is run by the teaching staff in the Digital technologies department. Each Digitech teacher is responsible for a range of games but each is only responsible for 1 game at a time. The choice of eSports games being offered is selected at the start of each year.

    • Mrs Cropp: Current responsible for League of Legends but also Valorant when it is offered
    • Mr Thomson: Currently responsible for Rocket League but also Project Cars when it is offered
    • Mr Gibson: Currently responsible for Minecraft but also Destiny 2 when it is offered

    Teams & Competitions

    Teams are organized into two categories:

    • Internal Teams: Those that play each other within school
    • External Teams: Those that represent CHS when competing against other schools

    Currently there are number of internal teams for each game currently competing on a casual basis. This is expected to become more of a house-based activity in the near future. As for external teams, we currently have 2 teams of senior players competing in the annual NZ League of Legends High School League, and 4 teams of Juniors competing in the Local Christchurch Minecraft competition. Recruitment for 2023 teams to compete in Valorant, Project Cars & Destiny 2 will star in November of this year.

    Rules

    All games typically follow the conventional gaming rules as specified by the International Esports Federation (iesf.org/governance/regulations) but in addition to these the following are applicable to CHS eSports teams and events. All participating students MUST:

    • Be achieving and up to date with school-based assessments and coursework expectations.
    • Have maintained at least a 90% attendance record for all classes.
    • Agree to represent the school in a positive manner.
    • Have provided written approval to compete from a parent or guardian.
